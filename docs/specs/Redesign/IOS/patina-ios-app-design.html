01 · Foundation

Design Principles

Five principles that govern every pixel of the Patina iOS experience. These aren't guidelines — they're the load-bearing walls of the entire interface.

01

Type Weight Replaces Containers

A product name in 500-weight Playfair at 26px tells you it's the headline. A maker tag in 10px DM Mono uppercase tells you it's metadata. No card needed. No shadow. No border.

02

Tactile Over Technical

Every interaction should feel like touching fabric swatches or wood samples. Haptic taps on capture. Magnetic snapping in AR. Cards that stack like material samples in hand.

03

Stories Over Specs

Lead with provenance and craftsmanship, not dimensions and SKUs. "Hand-turned in Ohio since 1904" appears before "24" W × 18" D × 30" H."

04

Journey Over Task

Frame scanning as "walking your room together," not "capture data." The 5-act structure (Invitation → Room Walk → Discovery → Living Room → Collection) transforms utility into narrative.

05

Whitespace as Architecture

Generous margins and padding create visual "rooms" where related information lives together — without needing explicit containers to define boundaries.

06

Layered Discovery

Reveal complexity gradually, like peeling back layers of patina. Primary info is bold and large. Supporting details lighter and smaller. Metadata monospaced and muted.

02 · Visual System

Color & Typography

The palette and type system that carries the entire interface. Earth-born colors. Three typefaces each with a clear purpose. No decoration — only information.

Navigation Architecture

Five Tabs, One Journey

The bottom tab bar uses the Patina icon system — 2px strokes, rounded corners, warmth in every glyph. Active states use Clay-filled backgrounds instead of color tints. The tab bar itself uses frosted glass over content.

Tab 1

Home

Discovery feed with artisan spotlights, curated collections, and the scan-prompt card. The front door of the experience.

Tab 2

Scan

Direct entry to room scanning. Opens camera with coaching overlay. Most sessions start here after onboarding.

Tab 3

For You

Personalized recommendation feed. Filtered by room, category, and budget. Evolves as the Aesthete Engine learns preferences.

Tab 4

Saved

Collections organized as mood boards. Pinterest-meets-catalog. Items can be grouped by room, project, or aesthetic mood.

Tab 5

Profile

Design journal with style evolution tracking, room scan history, and the gateway to designer consultation. A living document, not a settings page.

Interaction Patterns

Gestures & Feedback

Every gesture has meaning. Swipe right to save. Swipe left to pass. Hold for a material close-up. Haptic taps confirm captures. The app teaches these through gentle coaching — never through tutorials.

Gestures

Swipe Vocabulary

Right = Save (Love it). Left = Not for me (trains algorithm). Up = Share with someone. Down = Show me similar. Hold = Material close-up with texture detail.

Haptics

Tactile Feedback

Gentle tap on wall recognition during scan. Soft impact when furniture snaps to floor in AR. Spring bounce on save confirmation. No haptic on negative actions — silence is intentional.

Transitions

Movement Language

Cards slide forward from depth (vanishing point) on recommendation reveal. Quiz questions cross-dissolve with slight vertical offset. AR furniture fades in at 60% opacity, settling to 100%. Spring physics throughout — never linear.

Loading

Wait States

The Strata Mark animates as a loading indicator — three lines drawing left to right with staggered timing. "Taking a moment to curate..." replaces generic spinners. Progress is organic, like tea steeping.

Empty States

Blank Canvas

"A blank canvas — exciting!" for empty rooms. "Your collection starts here" for empty saves. Illustrations use the Patina illustration style — warm, hand-drawn, never clip-art.

Error States

Warm Recovery

Errors use Terracotta, not red. "Let's brighten things up a bit" for poor lighting. "Taking a moment to curate..." for network issues. Always conversational, never technical.

The type does the work.

Across all 18 screens, the hierarchy never relies on containers, card shadows, or colored backgrounds to communicate importance. Playfair carries the emotional weight. Inter does the utility work. DM Mono handles the metadata. The Strata Mark appears as a loading indicator, section divider, and brand signature — always three lines, always decreasing, always unmistakably Patina.

The five-act journey structure transforms a furniture shopping app into something that feels like walking through a design studio with a knowledgeable friend. Scanning is choreography. Discovery is curation. The collection is a design journal. And the maker stories remind you that behind every piece is a person with sawdust under their fingernails.

That's Patina. Where time adds value — in the furniture, the interface, and the relationship between the two.
