The Project PageEvery room, every item, every dollar, every day

A real interior design project isn't a six-step checklist. It's a living organism with 50–200 line items tracked across rooms, vendors, and phases. This design reflects how projects actually work — room-by-room scope, an eight-stage FF&E procurement pipeline per item, approval gates between phases, document version control, construction administration with RFIs and submittals, punch lists, time tracking, and financial reporting granular enough to know which throw pillow pushed the budget over.

Procurist · FF&E Schedules: The Complete Guide

What changed from v1.

Room-by-room scope replaced the flat list. Real projects organize by room — each with its own item count, budget allocation, and progress. The room cards show at a glance which spaces are furthest along and which need attention. The FF&E schedule groups by room, not just by vendor.

The FF&E schedule is now a real procurement pipeline. Every item tracks through eight stages: Specified → Quoted → Approved → Ordered → Production → Shipped → Delivered → Installed. Each stage has its own color-coded badge. Items blocked by pending decisions are immediately visible. The schedule is exportable — because vendors need spreadsheets, not portal links.

Approval gates control phase transitions. You can't enter Procurement without a signed proposal. You can't start Installation until all deliveries are confirmed. These gates appear inline on the phase timeline as starred callouts, making it clear what must be true before the project advances.

Financials show four columns, not two. Budget vs. Committed vs. Actual vs. Variance. "Committed" matters because a purchase order commits money before the item arrives. Variance is calculated per category and per room. The dining room is $1,330 over budget (scope change) while the living room is $4,760 under (still sourcing). The total project is under by $5,590 — but that's because several items haven't been ordered yet.

Documents have version control. The floor plan is at v3.2. The FF&E spec schedule is at v2.1 with 22 items. The proposal was signed at v1.1 after the scope expansion. Every document shows its version, last update date, and format. Upload new versions — the old ones stay accessible.

Time tracking connects effort to earnings. 47.5 hours logged across four phases, with an effective rate of $52.63/hour against the $2,500 design fee. This helps Leah understand her actual profitability per project — critical data for pricing future work.
