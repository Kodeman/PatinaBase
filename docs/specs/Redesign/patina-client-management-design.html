01 — The Client Lifecycle

Five stages, one continuous relationship.

A client isn't just a name on a project. They pass through five stages — each with different communication cadences, information needs, and decision points. The portal tracks where each client stands and surfaces the right tools for each stage.

Lead
Discovery

Anonymous → matched → accepted. Style profile, room scan, budget. Designer decides: accept, pass, or ask.

Response: 2–24h

Proposal
Courtship

Consultation, concept development, proposal sent. Client reviews, asks questions, signs or revises.

Cadence: 2–3× per week

Active
Partnership

Project underway. Decisions, approvals, procurement, installation. The deepest communication period.

Cadence: Weekly updates

Complete
Celebration

Final walkthrough, photography, review collection. The project becomes portfolio content and reputation capital.

Review request: Day 3

Nurture
Cultivation

Ongoing relationship. Seasonal check-ins, referral opportunities, future projects. Where repeat business grows.

Cadence: Quarterly

04 — Conversation Thread

Every message, in context.

Messages with a client live in one continuous thread — not scattered across email, text, and in-app. The thread shows the client's name and project context at the top. Decision requests and approval cards embed inline so clients can approve without leaving the conversation. Quick-reply templates sit above the compose bar for common responses.

06 — Decision Request

Ask clearly, make it easy to choose.

When Leah needs a client decision — rug color, fabric choice, layout approval — she creates a structured Decision Request that embeds in the conversation thread. Visual options with images, a clear deadline, and one-tap approval. Decisions are tracked on the client profile as pending, approved, or overdue.

Earn the review, grow the reputation.

The request includes specific questions about the experience. Collected reviews appear on Leah's public portfolio and improve her lead quality score on the platform.

08 — Relationship Nurture

Stay close without being clingy.

Completed clients move to the Nurture stage. The system suggests touchpoints — seasonal check-ins, anniversary messages, "thinking of you" notes when new products match their style. Leah controls which to send and can personalize each. The goal: every past client should feel remembered, not marketed to.

Design Principle — Relationships Are Compounding Assets

What makes this different.

The profile is a living story. Not a CRM record with fields — a narrative of the relationship from first match to latest message. The journey timeline shows every milestone. The style DNA captures what Leah has learned about this person's taste. The pending decisions surface what needs attention right now.

Decisions are designed objects. When a client needs to choose between two rug colors, they don't get a text message saying "which one?" They get a visual card with swatches, the designer's recommendation, context for why each works, and a one-tap selection. Structured decisions reduce back-and-forth and capture choices permanently.

Messages carry project context. Every conversation thread shows the project name and current phase. Quick-reply templates sit above the compose bar for common touchpoints — weekly updates, visit scheduling, decision requests. The designer never starts from a blank message; the system anticipates what she's likely sending.

Nurture is proactive, not reactive. The system watches for natural touchpoints — new products matching a past client's style, project anniversaries, seasonal timing — and suggests personalized notes. Leah controls what gets sent and personalizes each. The past client feels remembered. The referral pipeline stays warm.
