01 — The Decision Lifecycle

Every design project is a chain of choices.

A single room redesign involves 30 to 50 individual decisions — from anchor furniture down to hardware finishes. Today these decisions happen over email, text threads, and phone calls with no structured tracking. Patina makes each decision a first-class object with a clear lifecycle, visible across every part of the portal.

01 Create
Build the Choice

Designer defines the decision: title, context, visual options with swatches or images, her recommendation, and a deadline. Options include product choices, material swatches, color selections, layout approvals, or budget sign-offs.

02 Attach
Link to Context

Decision is linked to a project phase, a proposal section, or both. Linking enables the system to know which decisions block procurement, which gate phase advancement, and which are advisory.

03 Send
Deliver to Client

Decision card appears in the message thread. Client receives a notification. Visual options are tappable. Designer recommendation is flagged. Client can ask a question inline before choosing.

04 Track
Monitor Response

Decision appears on the project timeline, the client profile, and the decision dashboard. Status updates as the client views, asks questions, or lets the deadline approach. Overdue items surface automatically.

05 Resolve
Client Chooses

Client taps their selection. Optionally adds a note. Decision resolves with a timestamped record of what was chosen, by whom, and with what context — creating an immutable audit trail for the project.

06 Execute
Action Follows

Resolved decisions trigger downstream actions: product ordered in procurement, phase unblocked, proposal updated, task checked off. The choice flows directly into the work — no manual bridging required.

The client can ask questions above and below the card, then tap to select their choice without leaving the thread.

Design Principle — Decisions Deserve Structure

How decisions connect everything.

Decisions are objects, not messages. An email asking "which rug color?" disappears into a thread. A Patina decision is a structured card with visual options, a recommendation, a deadline, and a one-tap response. It persists on the client profile, the project timeline, and the decision dashboard simultaneously. It can't get lost.

Blocking decisions drive urgency. When a decision blocks procurement, the project timeline shows it. When it goes overdue, the dashboard surfaces it. The system doesn't nag the client — but it makes the impact visible to the designer, who can send a contextual reminder with "this is delaying your rug order by 2 weeks" instead of "hey, just checking in."

Resolution triggers action. When a client selects the Shaker chair, the system doesn't just log the choice. It unblocks the procurement task, updates the proposal line item, commits the budget, and notifies the designer to place the order. The moment of decision flows directly into the moment of execution. No copy-pasting between apps.

Analytics reveal patterns. Material decisions take 40% longer than product decisions. Budget changes take the longest of all. The Chen family responds in 2 days; the Whitfields need 5. These patterns aren't reports — they're ambient intelligence that helps Leah time her requests, batch related decisions, and anticipate bottlenecks before they stall a project.
