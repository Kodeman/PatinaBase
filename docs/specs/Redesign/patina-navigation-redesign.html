Patina — Navigation Redesign

Where Everything LivesNavigation that follows the work

The previous "Work | Clients | Studio" navigation was a reasonable first guess — three conceptual zones that felt clean. But after building ten detailed screen designs, the cracks show. Where do Products live? Where does the Aesthete Engine go? Why are Leads separate from Projects when they're the same pipeline? This document rebuilds the navigation from the designer's actual workflow, not from a feature taxonomy.

01 — What Broke

Three zones sounded right. Then we built the screens.

The "Work | Clients | Studio" model organized features into conceptual buckets. But real designer workflows don't follow buckets — they follow objects across contexts. A product gets captured, classified, added to a proposal, ordered in a project, and taught to the Aesthete Engine. It doesn't live in one zone. It passes through all of them.

Work

Leads, Proposals, Projects, Decisions, Room Viewer — everything about active work.

Problem: Leads and Projects are the same pipeline at different stages. Splitting them creates two places to check. Proposals are now structurally part of Projects (v2 redesign) but live under "Work" as a separate item. Products aren't here at all — but you need them constantly while working.

Clients

Client directory, Messages, Reviews, Nurture queue — everything about relationships.

Problem: Messages need to be accessible from any context — while working on a project, while reviewing a lead, while checking a product. Burying them under "Clients" means 2 clicks to respond to a time-sensitive message about a rug decision while you're looking at the rug in the product catalog.

Studio

Earnings, Portfolio, Resources, Settings — everything about the business.

Problem: The Aesthete Engine doesn't fit any zone. Teaching is "Work" (you're working). Products are "Work" (you use them in projects). But the Engine dashboard is "Studio" (it's about your practice). The Engine is its own workflow that crosses all three zones.

03 — The New Top Bar

Four primary zones. Three persistent utilities.

The navigation follows Leah's actual workflow: start with what needs attention (Today), move into the work (Pipeline), manage the materials (Products), tend the relationships (Clients). Messages, notifications, and search live in the utility bar because they interrupt any context. Business tools live under the profile because they're accessed occasionally, not constantly.

Today

The morning scan. What needs attention right now — new leads, unread messages, overdue decisions, upcoming milestones, deliveries arriving today, weekly updates due.

Replaces the "Dashboard" concept. Not metrics — action items. What do I do first today?

Pipeline

The entire lead-to-project lifecycle in one view. Leads, proposals, active projects, completed projects — all stages of the same pipeline. Sub-filtered by stage.

Replaces separate "Leads" + "Proposals" + "Projects" items. A lead IS a project at the earliest stage. A proposal IS a project being defined.

Products

The full product world — catalog browsing, capture queue from the Chrome extension, teaching sessions, Aesthete Engine intelligence, and product detail pages.

Gets its own primary nav slot because product work is a daily workflow, not a sub-task. Searching for a coffee table is a 20-minute activity, not a sidebar click.

Clients

The relationship directory. Client profiles, reviews, nurture queue, referral tracking. The people view — who are my clients, how are the relationships, who needs attention.

Messages are NOT here. They're promoted to the utility bar. Clients is about relationship management, not real-time communication.

The Utility Bar (Always Visible)
⌕ Search

Global search across projects, products, clients, and documents. Opens as a command palette (⌘K). Type anything — the system finds it.

◎ Notifications

Deliveries, vendor confirmations, client actions (signed, viewed), system alerts. Badge shows unread count. Opens as dropdown.

◆ Messages

All client conversations. Unread count always visible. Opens message panel from any page — respond without leaving current context.

LK Profile

Dropdown: Earnings, Portfolio, Time Tracking, Settings, Help. Business tools accessed occasionally, not constantly.

04 — Contextual Sub-Navigation

Each zone gets its own filter bar.

Below the top bar, each primary zone shows a contextual sub-navigation that filters its content. These are not tabs — they're pipeline stage filters. The content below changes; the page doesn't reload. Breadcrumbs appear when you're deep inside a specific item.

05 — Mobile Navigation

Four tabs at the bottom. Messages always visible.

On mobile, the four primary zones become bottom tab bar icons. Messages gets a fifth tab because it's the most time-sensitive action on mobile — Leah needs to respond to a client decision while standing in the paint aisle. Search is a pull-down gesture from any screen. Notifications live under the bell in Today.

Mobile Differences
Messages gets its own tab. On desktop, Messages is a utility-bar icon. On mobile, it's a full tab because responding to clients is the most common mobile action. Leah checks messages between site visits, in the car, at the store.
Search is pull-down. Pull down from the top of any screen to reveal the search bar. No icon in the tab bar — that space is too valuable.
Notifications fold into Today. The Today tab already shows what needs attention. Notifications stream into the same feed — no separate bell icon needed.
Profile lives in Today's header. Tap the greeting ("Good morning, Leah") to access Earnings, Portfolio, Settings. Not a separate tab.

06 — Profile Menu

Business tools, not the main event.

Earnings, Portfolio, Time Tracking, and Settings are used weekly, not hourly. They don't deserve primary navigation slots. They live under the profile avatar as a dropdown — always accessible, never competing with the active work for attention.

"A lead is a project you haven't started yet. A proposal is a project you're defining. An active project is a proposal someone signed. A completed project is one you're closing. They're not four separate things — they're one pipeline at different stages."
Navigation Principle — One Pipeline, Not Four Destinations

Why this works better.

Pipeline unifies the lifecycle. Leads, proposals, active projects, and completed projects are the same list filtered by stage. A lead you accept today appears as a proposal draft tomorrow and an active project next month without ever changing navigation contexts. The colored dots (blue → gold → clay → sage) show progression at a glance.

Products earn their own lane. In the old model, products had no home — they were accessed from within projects or proposals as a sidebar action. But real product work is a substantial daily activity: browsing the catalog, classifying new imports, teaching the Aesthete Engine, reviewing match corrections. It deserves a primary nav position with its own sub-navigation: Catalog, Capture Queue, Teaching, Aesthete Engine.

Messages are ambient, not buried. Client communication is the most time-sensitive action in the portal. Burying it under "Clients" added an extra click to every message response. Promoting it to the utility bar means Leah can respond from any page — while looking at a product, while reviewing a project, while on the Today dashboard. The unread count is always visible. The message panel slides open without losing context.

Business tools are in the right place. Earnings, Portfolio, Time Tracking, and Settings aren't daily-driver tools. They're checked weekly or when a specific question arises. The profile dropdown puts them 2 clicks away — accessible but not consuming primary navigation real estate that the actual work needs.
