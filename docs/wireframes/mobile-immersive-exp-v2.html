Reimagining Patina as an immersive companion experience — breaking from conventional mobile patterns to create something that honors how discovery actually feels.

The Challenge

What We're Leaving Behind

Conventional mobile patterns treat discovery like a transaction. Swipe left, swipe right. Tap here, tap there. But finding furniture that will age with you isn't a transaction — it's a relationship.

❌ Conventional Patterns

  • ✕ Carousels that rush you through "onboarding"
  • ✕ Multiple-choice quizzes that reduce taste to checkboxes
  • ✕ AR scanning that feels clinical and technical
  • ✕ Swipe cards that gamify decision-making
  • ✕ Product grids that commoditize craft
  • ✕ Tab bars that fragment the journey

✓ Patina Patterns

  • ✓ A single threshold moment that invites you in
  • ✓ A conversation that learns your language
  • ✓ A meditative walk that appreciates your space
  • ✓ Pieces that emerge and tell their stories
  • ✓ A craftsman's table where collections live
  • ✓ A companion presence, always available

Core Philosophy

Time Is the Medium

If Patina believes that time adds value, then the app itself must honor time. Not rush it. Not compress it. Not gamify it away.

Presence Over Progress

No progress bars racing toward completion. Instead, moments that reward your attention. The longer you look, the more you see.

Conversation Over Selection

Don't ask "which one?" Ask "tell me more." Understanding isn't multiple choice — it's an ongoing dialogue.

Emergence Over Discovery

Products don't wait in a catalog. They surface when the time is right, like memories, like ideas that find you.

Accumulation Over Collection

Your saved pieces aren't a checklist. They're layers that develop character together, like patina itself.

Interaction Design

A New Gesture Language

Every interaction should feel like handling materials — not pressing buttons. These gestures replace conventional taps and swipes.

🤲

Hold

Gentle, sustained contact. Like holding something precious. Used to cross thresholds, to show you're present and ready.

🫳

Pull

Like opening a drawer. Reveals depth — more information, the companion, hidden layers. Feels physical, not digital.

🫴

Place

Drag and release, like setting something down on a table. Items settle with physics. Position matters.

👆

Linger

Rest your finger on something to reveal more. The longer you linger, the more is revealed. Patience is rewarded.

🤏

Pinch In

Bring things together. Used to combine, to see relationships, to ask "how do these work together?" A gesture of synthesis.

🙌

Release

Let something drift away. Not a rejection — a gentle release. "Not now." The item fades gracefully, might return later.

Implementation Notes

What This Means for Development

Technical Implications

  • → NLP-powered conversation engine (not branching quiz)
  • → Time-based UI elements (day/night cycles, aging effects)
  • → Physics-based collection interface (drag, settle, stack)
  • → Intent-based navigation ("Show me my rooms")
  • → Sophisticated emergence algorithm (timing, relevance)

UX Research Needs

  • → Gesture usability testing (hold, pull, linger)
  • → Pacing validation (how slow is too slow?)
  • → Conversation tone testing with target users
  • → Accessibility adaptations for new patterns
  • → A/B test conventional vs. companion navigation
