Navigation & Flow Specification

The Companion
System

How users flow through Patina without tabs, menus, or conventional navigation — guided by a breathing presence that knows where they are and where they want to go.

Core Concept

Navigation as Conversation

The Companion replaces traditional navigation entirely. There's no tab bar, no hamburger menu, no bottom navigation. Instead, a single, living element — the Strata Mark — is always present, ready to guide.

"Where do you want to go?" becomes "What would you like to do?" Navigation through intent, not icons.

— Design philosophy

🧭

Always Present

The Companion appears on every screen. It's never hidden, never replaced. A constant, reassuring presence.

💬

Intent-Based

"Show me my rooms." "What surfaced today?" Users describe where they want to go. Patina understands and responds.

🎯

Context-Aware

The Companion knows which screen you're on. Pull it up from Emergence, and it knows you might want to discuss that piece.

"I want to see that chair again" → Specific piece detail

"Start over" → Conversation reset
"I need help" → Guidance overlay

Transition Behavior

1 Smooth collapse: When navigating away, the Companion smoothly collapses as the new screen slides in. Never abrupt.
2 Context preservation: The Companion remembers the conversation. Return to it and pick up where you left off.
3 Graceful uncertainty: If Patina isn't sure what you mean, it asks for clarification rather than guessing wrong.
4 Back gestures: Edge swipe back always works as expected, returning to the previous screen in the stack.

Interaction Design

Companion Gestures

Physical gestures that feel natural and embodied — like reaching for a friend rather than pressing a button.

🫳

Pull Up

Drag upward from the Strata Mark. Quick actions appear at 80px. Full conversation opens at 150px. Spring physics provide satisfying feedback.

👇

Push Down

Drag down on the expanded Companion to collapse. Also triggered by tapping outside the sheet. Conversation state is preserved.

👆

Tap Mark

Quick tap expands to full conversation immediately. Useful when you know you want to talk, not just peek at quick actions.

🎤

Long Press

Hold on the Strata Mark to activate voice input. Speak your intent naturally. Release to send. Haptic confirms activation.

Companion State Machine

State Height Triggers Next States
collapsed 80px Default, push down, tap outside, navigation pulling, expanded

expanded 400px Pull past threshold, tap mark, notification tap collapsed, navigating
navigating Collapsing User requests navigation via intent collapsed (on new screen)
pulsing 80px New emergence or insight available expanded (on interact), collapsed (message viewed)

Companion Voice

How Patina Speaks

The Companion has a consistent voice — warm, curious, unhurried. Never robotic, never salesy.

"I noticed you've lingered on that chair. There's something about hand-turned wood that wants to be touched, isn't there?"

— Contextual observation

Voice Principles

✓ Uses "I" sparingly: "I noticed" not "I think you should"
✓ Asks, doesn't tell: "Would you like to..." not "You should..."
✓ Specific, not generic: Names pieces, makers, materials
✓ Sensory language: Touch, warmth, weight, grain

Sample Phrases

💬 "Something surfaced that might speak to that corner you mentioned."
💬 "The light in here is remarkable. Afternoon sun?"
💬 "These two pieces have been on your table awhile. They complement each other."
💬 "Ready when you are. No rush."
